Achivements

Dr. Manmohan Singh, renowned economist and politician, served as India’s Prime Minister from 2004 to 2014 and is celebrated for his transformative contributions to India’s economy and governance. As Finance Minister in 1991, he spearheaded the landmark economic liberalization that opened India to global markets, dismantling the restrictive license raj, reducing tariffs, and fostering foreign investment, which averted a balance of payments crisis and set India on a path of sustained growth. During his tenure as Prime Minister, India witnessed remarkable economic expansion, averaging 8% growth during his first term, and became the world’s third-largest economy in purchasing power parity terms. His government introduced transformative social welfare programs such as the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA), Right to Education Act, and Right to Information Act, significantly improving rural livelihoods, education, and governance transparency. Additionally, his leadership saw major infrastructure advancements, including highway modernization and rural electrification, which bolstered connectivity and economic opportunities across the nation. Dr. Singh’s legacy lies in his role as a visionary economist and a statesman who shaped modern India.
