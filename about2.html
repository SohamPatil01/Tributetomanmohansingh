Policies

Dr. Manmohan Singh's tenure as both Finance Minister and Prime Minister was marked by transformative policies that reshaped India’s economy and society. As Finance Minister in 1991, he introduced groundbreaking economic reforms, including liberalization, privatization, and globalization, which dismantled restrictive controls, opened India to foreign investment, and set the stage for sustained growth. As Prime Minister, his government focused on social welfare, launching landmark initiatives like the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) for rural livelihoods, the Right to Education Act (RTE) for universal education, and the Food Security Act to ensure subsidized food for the poor. His leadership emphasized rural development through the Bharat Nirman program and urban modernization via the Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewal Mission (JNNURM). Dr. Singh also strengthened transparency with the Right to Information Act and improved healthcare access through the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM). On the infrastructure front, he expanded national highways and electrification efforts. His economic vision extended to global relations, highlighted by the India-U.S. Civil Nuclear Agreement, which bolstered energy security. Singh’s policies reflected a balance between economic growth and inclusive development, solidifying his legacy as a reformist leader.
